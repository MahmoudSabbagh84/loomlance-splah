## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Would a discerning eye say "AI/template made this"? Partly, yes — not because it's broken, but because every choice is the *modal* dev-tool landing page.** Dark hero + violet radial glow + a 6-card feature grid with rounded icon tiles + "Built by devs who get it" + dark CTA band. Each move is defensible in isolation; together they're the 2026 SaaS template. The brand register's bar is *distinctiveness* ("average is no longer findable"), and this hasn't yet risked a memorable POV.

**Deterministic scan:** `single-font` on **every** page (only Outfit — a reflex-default face, flagged in the brand register) and `em-dash-overuse` (index 8, pricing 9 — an AI cadence tell). Two specific brand-bans are present in the markup: **identical card grids** (the 6 feature cards) and **large rounded icon tiles above every heading**.

**Real renders confirm:** the composition is competent and clean at both breakpoints; nothing is visually broken. The flatness is in the *voice*, not the execution.

## Overall Impression
This is a genuinely solid, trustworthy marketing site — cohesive with the app, fast, with production-grade forms and honest pricing. It will convert. The single biggest opportunity is **personality**: right now it could be any dev SaaS. A distinctive headline typeface, a varied (non-grid) feature layout, and a *real* product screenshot would move it from "clean and safe" to "memorable." And mobile navigation is a real functional gap to close first.

## What's Working
- **Cohesion with the product.** The splash and the app share palette, type, and components — a visitor feels no seam between `loomlance.com` and `app.loomlance.com`. That's a real, deliberate strength.
- **Pricing page.** The strongest page: clear tiers with a featured "Most popular" Freelancer, a monthly/annual toggle with a "2 months free" incentive, honest "what's not included" rows, and a thorough 6-item FAQ. This is conversion craft.
- **Production-grade forms.** Signup (plan selector with `?plan=` deep-link, live password-requirement checklist, show/hide, confirm-match, `role=radiogroup`) and contact (honeypot, 1-min rate-limit, char counter, real SES delivery, inline validation + `role=alert`). Far beyond typical marketing-site forms.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] No mobile navigation.** On every page the header links (`Features / Pricing / About / Contact`) are `hidden sm:block` with **no hamburger/menu**. On a phone the header collapses to just "Sign in" + "Get started" — Features/Pricing/About/Contact are unreachable from the nav (only via footer). For a marketing site where mobile is a large share of first-touch traffic, this strands visitors. **Fix:** add a mobile hamburger → slide-in menu (mirror the dashboard's `MobileNav`). **Command:** `/impeccable adapt`.
- **[P1] Brand distinctiveness — it reads as a safe template.** The home page makes only modal choices: dark-hero-violet-glow, 6 identical icon-tile feature cards, single geometric sans. Per the brand register this is the slop ceiling. **Fix:** introduce a distinctive *display* typeface for headlines (keep Outfit for body, preserving app cohesion); break the 6-identical-card grid into a varied, weighted feature layout; give the hero a real point of view. **Command:** `/impeccable typeset` then `/impeccable bolder` (home).
- **[P2] The hero "product preview" is a CSS mock, not a real screenshot.** It's hand-built divs ($8,450, fake activity rows). The brand register explicitly says don't replace product imagery with CSS panels — and you have the *actual* dashboard to screenshot. A real (even lightly stylized) app screenshot is more convincing and can't drift from reality. **Fix:** drop in a real dashboard screenshot. **Command:** `/impeccable bolder`.
- **[P2] Terms of Service & Privacy Policy don't exist.** Signup's required consent checkbox links to `alert('coming soon')`. For a live site collecting PII + taking payments, missing legal pages is a trust and compliance gap. **Fix:** ship real `/terms` and `/privacy` pages (or a minimal version). **Command:** `/impeccable craft`.
- **[P2] Copy cadence: em-dash overuse.** 8–9 em-dashes per page in body copy reads as AI rhythm. **Fix:** vary punctuation (commas, periods, parentheses). **Command:** `/impeccable clarify`.

## Persona Red Flags
**Casey (distracted mobile):** the headline gap — lands on the home page on a phone, wants Pricing, and the nav has no Pricing link (no menu). Must scroll the entire page to the footer to navigate. High bounce risk on the exact device most marketing visitors use.

**Jordan (first-timer):** served well on desktop — clear hero, obvious "Start free trial," readable pricing + FAQ. But the faux dashboard mock may register as "not a real product yet," and the "(soon)" tags across pricing features could read as "half-built."

**Riley (stress tester):** forms are robust (honeypot, rate-limit, validation, real delivery) — hard to break. Will notice the Terms/Privacy links are dead `alert()` stubs and that the consent checkbox gates on documents that don't exist.

## Minor Observations
- Pricing shows **4 tiers** (Solo/Freelancer/Studio/Agency); the ideal is ≤3, but Agency is correctly de-emphasized (dashed, "Coming soon"), so it's acceptable.
- Pricing's excluded-feature rows use `text-fg-subtle` (sub-AA) for meaningful "not included" text — borderline contrast.
- Signup/signin are intentionally chrome-less (focused conversion) while index/pricing/contact carry the nav — a reasonable convention, just an intentional inconsistency to be aware of.
- `signin.html` is a thin redirect to the dashboard login — correct (unified auth), nothing to critique visually.

## Questions to Consider
- What's the one-sentence brand POV that *isn't* "developer-first freelance platform"? If the headline could only run on LoomLance and no competitor, what would it say?
- If the hero showed the *real* dashboard instead of a mock, would the whole page feel more credible — and could that screenshot become the page's signature image?
- Does "one product" cohesion require the *marketing* site to use the same single sans as the app, or can headlines carry a distinct display face while the body stays Outfit?
